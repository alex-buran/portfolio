I

The Barrier Forms

The scene is a short looping video, mapped onto a plane that's parented to the camera so it always fills the frame exactly, at any window size, with no stretching. Scrolling doesn't move a camera through space — it drives a shader-side zoom that pushes deeper into the gate, frame by frame.

II

A Frame, Not a Photo

The fragment shader fits the video into the frame by hand — a per-pixel "background-size: cover" equivalent that compares screen aspect to video aspect before sampling, so the gate never warps or letterboxes as the window resizes.

III

Zoom Without a Camera Move

Scrolling drives a shader-side zoom that scales the video's UV coordinates around the frame's center, pushing into the gate without moving any real geometry toward it — the entire scroll journey lives in this one effect, end to end.

IV

Inside the Maw

Pointer movement nudges the shader's UV offset, adding a parallax tilt on top of motion that's already real — the water and the red aura drift are baked into the video itself, not faked in the shader. A darken pass, a distance-based vignette, and a faint warm color grade run on top of every frame, all in GLSL, grading the footage rather than replacing it.

"Malevolent Shrine" is a non-commercial fan project depicting Sukuna's Domain Expansion from Jujutsu Kaisen by Gege Akutami, including the character's likeness and the domain's name in its original script. Built for portfolio purposes only — not for sale, not monetized, no affiliation with the original creators or publishers.
